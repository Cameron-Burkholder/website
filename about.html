My Story

I started learning to code in 7th grade, when I saw an ad on Facebook for a platform that taught people how to code for free in exchange for working with nonprofit organizations. I started working through this platform, among others, and racked up hundreds of hours of coding by the time I was a high school freshman.

In my sophomore year of high school, I started to built websites for local businesses. First, my father's accounting firm, then a lady from my church wanted to sell her book about her cancer experiences. By the time I graduated I had built about 10 websites for small businesses near me, and my career goals changed from engineering to computer science.

I enjoy learning and building projects. So far I've built a handful of smaller web apps for certifications. You can check those out in my portfolio. In addition, I'm building larger applications for things that interest me. I play guitar, so I'm building a guitar practice management tool. I play R6 Siege with my friends, so I'm building a platform for managing teams and strategies.

I'm open to new projects and working with new people, so if you have any questions reach out.
